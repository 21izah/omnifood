Buying and selling cryptocurrencies involves exchanging digital assets, such as Bitcoin or Ether, for fiat currency or other cryptocurrencies. This can be done through online exchanges, crypto ATMs, or other methods. When buying crypto, you're essentially exchanging fiat currency for a digital asset, which is then stored in a digital wallet. Selling crypto involves the reverse process, where you exchange your digital asset for fiat currency or another cryptocurrency. Fees are typically associated with these transactions, and prices can fluctuate rapidly due to market volatility. It's essential to research and understand the fees, risks, and market conditions before engaging in crypto transactions.
